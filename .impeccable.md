## Design Context

### Users
ClawPanel is used by technically capable operators, developers, and AI-agent administrators who need to manage OpenClaw, channels, plugins, workflows, sessions, tasks, logs, updates, and external runtimes from one lightweight panel. They use it in setup, troubleshooting, monitoring, and day-to-day operations where clarity, confidence, and fast diagnosis matter more than decorative novelty.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel like an industrial operations console: precise, reliable, disciplined, and quietly powerful. The tone should communicate operational confidence and local control, not consumer SaaS gloss or generic AI futurism.

### Aesthetic Direction
Primary direction: industrial console. Use a dual-theme system with light and dark modes sharing the same design tokens. Avoid the common AI palette of cyan/purple neon, heavy glassmorphism, glowing gradients, and generic rounded cards. Prefer tinted neutral surfaces, strong information hierarchy, crisp separators, restrained accent colors, and purposeful status language. Page-level re-layout is allowed as long as business logic, API calls, routing, state, and data semantics remain unchanged.

### Design Principles
1. UI-only changes: preserve business logic, state flow, API contracts, route behavior, and existing data dependencies.
2. Console clarity over decoration: make status, controls, and risk boundaries immediately scannable.
3. Tokenized dual-theme design: centralize colors, surfaces, borders, focus rings, spacing rhythm, and shadows in CSS tokens.
4. Reduce card monotony: use panels, rails, tables, dense groups, and asymmetric compositions instead of wrapping everything in identical cards.
5. Operational accessibility: maintain readable contrast, visible focus states, reduced-motion safety, responsive layouts, and no hidden critical controls.
